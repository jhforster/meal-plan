Thursday Pasta bake & lemony collards 10 min active · reheat dinner
Low-lift night The kids are home during the day, so keep this to reheating plus one quick green side.

Ingredients

  • Prepared pasta bake1 tray or portions
  • Extra collard greens, chopped
  • Garlic, thinly sliced or minced2 cloves
  • Lemon juice or apple cider vinegar
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper

Do this

  1. Reheat the pasta bake covered, either in the oven or microwave.
  2. Warm olive oil in a skillet. Add garlic for 30 seconds, then add the collards and a pinch of salt.
  3. Cook until tender, adding a splash of water if the pan gets dry.
  4. Finish the greens with lemon juice or cider vinegar and black pepper.
Saturday Mustardy sausage, cabbage & salt potatoes 25 min active · 40 min total
Before serving Reserve about 2 lb cooked potatoes for Monday’s cheesesteak skillet.

Ingredients

  • Salt potatoes5 lb bag
  • Sweet sausage4 links
  • Spicy sausage4 links
  • Green cabbage, cut into wedges or thick slicesabout half the head
  • Dijon mustard1/4 cup
  • Apple cider vinegar or lemon juice3 tbsp
  • Honey1 tbsp
  • Fresh dill, choppedoptional but good
  • Butter or olive oil, salt, pepper

Do this

  1. Thaw sausage ahead. Move it to the fridge Friday or Saturday morning.
  2. Cook the full bag of salt potatoes in a large pot according to the bag directions. Drain and toss with butter or olive oil.
  3. Whisk Dijon, vinegar or lemon, honey, dill, black pepper, and enough olive oil to loosen it. Keep the sauce on the side for the kids.
  4. Grill or skillet-cook the sweet and spicy sausages, keeping them easy to tell apart.
  5. Grill the cabbage wedges or sear them in a wide skillet until browned and tender-crisp. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve sausage, cabbage, and potatoes with the mustard sauce. Reserve Monday’s potatoes before seconds.
Saturday prep Reserve Monday’s potatoes 2 min · chill for skillet

Do this

  1. Before serving or right after dinner, set aside about 2 lb cooked salt potatoes.
  2. Cool, cover, and refrigerate. They will crisp better Monday if they are cold.

Use them in Monday’s cheesesteak potato skillet

Sunday Ginger-soy pork bowls 30 min active · reserve for Wednesday
Cook in batches Three pounds of ground pork will brown better in two skillets or two batches.

Ingredients

  • Ground pork3 lb
  • Rice3 cups dry
  • Frozen in-shell edamame
  • Green cabbage, thinly slicedremaining half head
  • Cucumbers, sliced2
  • Scallions, sliced1 bunch
  • Garlic, minced4 cloves
  • Fresh ginger, grated2 tbsp
  • Soy sauce1/2 cup
  • Rice vinegar1/4 cup
  • Brown sugar or honey2 tbsp
  • Sesame oil1 tbsp
  • Lime juice, chili crispfor serving

Do this

  1. Start the rice. Cook enough to serve tonight and reserve 4 cups cooked rice for Wednesday.
  2. Stir together soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar or honey, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Brown the pork in two skillets or in batches. Pour off excess fat if needed.
  4. Add the sauce and simmer 2–3 minutes, until glossy and well-coated.
  5. Steam the edamame. Toss cabbage, cucumbers, and scallions with lime juice, a little rice vinegar, salt, and a drizzle of sesame oil.
  6. Serve pork over rice with edamame and crunchy vegetables. Add chili crisp at the table.
Sunday prep Reserve Wednesday’s pork & rice 3 min · chill uncovered first

Do this

  1. Set aside about 1 lb cooked pork and 4 cups cooked rice before serving dinner.
  2. Spread the rice in a shallow container so it cools quickly, then cover and refrigerate.
  3. Refrigerate the pork separately so Wednesday’s fried rice comes together fast.

Use this base in Wednesday’s fried rice

Monday Cheesesteak potato skillet 20 min active · one-shot dinner

Ingredients

  • Reserved cooked salt potatoes, smashed or halvedabout 2 lb
  • Frozen Steak-ummsabout 1½–2 lb
  • Bell peppers, sliced2
  • Onion, sliced1
  • Provolone6–8 slices
  • Open jar marinarafor spooning or dipping
  • Olive oil, garlic powder, oregano, salt, pepper

Do this

  1. Crisp the cold potatoes in a large skillet with oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Move to a plate.
  2. Cook peppers and onions in the same skillet until browned and tender.
  3. Add Steak-umms and cook, breaking them up as they soften. Season lightly with oregano and pepper.
  4. Return potatoes to the skillet. Top with provolone and cover briefly until melted.
  5. Warm marinara and serve it spooned over adult portions or alongside for dipping.

Tuesday Dumplings & cucumber salad 15–20 min · quick night

Ingredients

  • Frozen dumplings
  • Cucumbers, thinly sliced2
  • Frozen in-shell edamameoptional side
  • Cooked riceoptional side
  • Fruit or raw bell peppersoptional kid side
  • Soy sauce3 tbsp
  • Rice vinegar2 tbsp
  • Sesame oil1 tsp
  • Honey or sugar1 tsp
  • Sesame seeds, scallions, chili crispoptional

Do this

  1. Cook dumplings from frozen according to the package. Steam-and-sear is ideal if the package gives that option.
  2. Whisk soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and honey or sugar.
  3. Toss cucumbers with the dressing. Add sesame seeds and scallions if using.
  4. Heat edamame, rice, fruit, or raw vegetables as needed so dinner feels complete without much work.
  5. Serve chili crisp or extra soy-sesame dressing for adults.
Wednesday Pork & edamame fried rice 20 min active · reserved base

Ingredients

  • Reserved cooked rice, coldabout 4 cups
  • Reserved ginger-soy porkabout 1 lb
  • Eggs4
  • Edamame, shelled after steaming1–2 cups
  • Scallions, sliced
  • Soy sauce2–3 tbsp
  • Rice vinegar1 tbsp
  • Sesame oil1 tsp
  • Chili crispfor adults

Do this

  1. Scramble eggs in a hot skillet or wok, then move them to a plate.
  2. Add oil and the cold rice. Let it sit briefly so some grains crisp, then stir-fry.
  3. Add pork and edamame; cook until hot.
  4. Season with soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Fold eggs back in.
  5. Finish with scallions. Add chili crisp at the table.
