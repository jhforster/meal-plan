Thursday Sheet-pan lemon-garlic chicken 20 min active · 35 min total
Before serving Reserve enough chicken and sweet potatoes for Sunday’s wraps.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs, thawedabout 4 lb
  • Sweet potatoes, 1-inch piecesabout 3 lb
  • Brussels sprouts, halved2 lb
  • Lemons2
  • Garlic, minced4–5 cloves
  • Arugulahalf the container
  • Olive oil, oregano, garlic powder, salt, pepper

Do this

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Line two sheet pans.
  2. Toss sweet potatoes and Brussels sprouts with oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Toss chicken with oil, juice of one lemon, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Divide between pans. Roast 25–35 minutes, rotating halfway, until the chicken is cooked and vegetables are browned.
  5. Dress arugula with oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and Parmesan if using.
Saturday White bean & ham hock soup 20 min active · slow cook
Friday night If using dried beans, cover them with plenty of water and soak overnight.

Ingredients

  • Cross-cut ham hocks
  • Dried white beans, soaked, or 6 cans drained2 lb
  • Onions, diced2
  • Carrots, diced4
  • Celery stalks, diced4
  • Garlic, minced4–5 cloves
  • Prepared chicken broth8 cups
  • Kale or collards, chopped1 large bunch
  • Bay leaves, thyme, black pepper

Do this

  1. Sauté onions, carrots, and celery in oil for 8 minutes. Add garlic for the final minute.
  2. Add ham hocks, beans, broth, bay leaves, thyme, pepper, and enough water to cover.
  3. Cook until tender: Instant Pot 45 minutes plus natural release; slow cooker 8–10 hours on low; or stovetop 2–3 hours.
  4. Remove ham hocks. Shred the usable meat and return it to the soup.
  5. Stir in greens and cook until tender. Taste before adding salt.
  6. Prepare cornbread according to its package.
After dinner Refrigerate enough for Monday and lunches. Freeze the rest in meal-sized portions.

Sunday prep Assemble Tuesday’s baked pasta 25 min active · refrigerate until Tuesday
Do ahead Assemble this Sunday so Tuesday only requires baking.

Ingredients

  • Rigatoni plus penne2 lb total
  • Mild Italian sausage2 lb
  • Onion, diced1
  • Spinach8 oz
  • Marinara2 jars
  • Ricotta15 oz
  • Shredded mozzarella1 lb
  • Parmesan

Do this

  1. Cook pasta 2 minutes less than its package directions. Drain.
  2. Brown sausage and onion. Drain excess grease, then wilt in the spinach.
  3. Mix pasta, sausage mixture, marinara, ricotta, and half the mozzarella.
  4. Transfer to a large baking dish. Top with remaining mozzarella and Parmesan.
  5. Cover and refrigerate until Tuesday.
Monday Soup & grilled cheese 15 min · reheat
  1. Reheat Saturday’s soup gently, adding a little water or broth if it has thickened.

  3. Freeze any soup that will not be eaten by Tuesday.

  3. Uncover and bake another 10–15 minutes, until bubbling and browned.
  4. Reserve enough for Wednesday dinner and lunches.
