Thursday Sheet-pan lemon-garlic chicken 20 min active · 35 min total
Before serving Reserve enough chicken and sweet potatoes for Sunday’s wraps.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs, thawedabout 4 lb
  • Sweet potatoes, 1-inch piecesabout 3 lb
  • Brussels sprouts, halved2 lb
  • Lemons2
  • Garlic, minced4–5 cloves
  • Arugulahalf the container
  • Olive oil, oregano, garlic powder, salt, pepper

Do this

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Line two sheet pans.
  2. Toss sweet potatoes and Brussels sprouts with oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Toss chicken with oil, juice of one lemon, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Divide between pans. Roast 25–35 minutes, rotating halfway, until the chicken is cooked and vegetables are browned.
  5. Dress arugula with oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and Parmesan if using.
Saturday Beach wraps & quesadilla wedges 20 min · pack in a cooler

Ingredients

  • Reserved cooked chicken, chopped
  • Reserved roasted sweet potatoes
  • Arugula
  • Tortillas
  • Cheese
  • Honey mustard or ranch
  • Raw vegetables, fruit, ranchoptional sides

Adult wraps

  1. Fill tortillas with chopped chicken, sweet potatoes, arugula, cheese, and a light amount of sauce.
  2. Wrap tightly and cut in half. Keep extra sauce separate so the wraps do not get soggy.

Kid quesadilla wedges

  1. Fill tortillas with finely chopped chicken and cheese. Add a thin layer of mashed sweet potato if wanted.
  2. Pan-sear until crisp, cool slightly, and cut into easy-to-hold wedges.
Pack cold Keep wraps, quesadillas, cheese, chicken, and sauces in a cooler. Do not leave them unrefrigerated for more than two hours.
Saturday prep Assemble Sunday’s baked pasta 25 min active · refrigerate overnight
Do ahead This batch needs two 9×13-inch baking dishes. Assemble both today so Sunday only requires baking.

Ingredients

  • Rigatoni plus penne2 lb total
  • Mild Italian sausage2 lb
  • Onion, diced1
  • Spinach8 oz
  • Marinara2 jars
  • Ricotta15 oz
  • Shredded mozzarella1 lb
  • Parmesan

Do this

  1. Cook pasta 2 minutes less than its package directions. Drain.
  2. Spread about 1 cup from the second marinara jar across the bottoms of two 9×13-inch dishes.
  3. Brown sausage and onion. Drain excess grease, wilt in the spinach, then stir in the entire first marinara jar.
  4. Mix pasta, sauced sausage, ricotta, and half the mozzarella. Divide between the dishes.
  5. Spoon the remaining second marinara jar over the pasta. Top with remaining mozzarella and Parmesan.
  6. Cover and refrigerate until Sunday.
Saturday night Store soaked beans for Monday 5 min · refrigerate
After 8–12 hours soaking Drain and rinse the beans, then refrigerate promptly in a covered container at 40°F or colder.
  1. Drain and rinse the soaked beans thoroughly.
  2. Transfer to a covered container and refrigerate until Monday.
  3. Before cooking, discard the beans if they smell sour, feel slimy, or show unexpected bubbling.
Sunday Sausage & ricotta baked pasta 10 min active · 45 min total
  1. Take Saturday’s assembled pasta out while the oven heats to 375°F.
  2. Bake both dishes covered for about 30 minutes.
  3. Uncover and bake another 10–15 minutes, until bubbling and browned.
  4. Reserve enough pasta for Tuesday dinner and adult lunches.

Monday White bean & ham hock soup 20 min active · start early
Use all the collards The entire bunch goes into the soup. There are no leftover greens reserved for another dinner.

Ingredients

  • Cross-cut smoked ham hocks2½–3 lb / about 4–6 pieces
  • Soaked white beans, drained and refrigerated2 lb dry before soaking
  • Onions, diced2
  • Carrots, diced4
  • Celery stalks, diced4
  • Garlic, minced4–5 cloves
  • Prepared chicken broth8 cups
  • Collard greens, choppedentire large bunch
  • Cornbread mix2 boxes
  • Jalapeños, choppedfor 1 cornbread
  • Bay leaves, thyme, black pepper

Do this

  1. Sauté onions, carrots, and celery in oil for 8 minutes. Add garlic for the final minute.
  2. Add ham hocks, beans, broth, bay leaves, thyme, pepper, and enough water to cover.
  3. Cook until tender: Instant Pot for 20–25 minutes plus natural release, or gently simmer on the stovetop for about 2–3 hours. Add more time if the ham hocks are not yet tender.
  4. Remove ham hocks. Shred the usable meat and return it to the soup.
  5. Stir in the entire bunch of collards and cook until tender. Taste before adding salt.
  6. Prepare both boxes of cornbread mix. Leave one plain for the kids and stir chopped jalapeños into the other before baking.
After dinner Refrigerate enough soup for Wednesday and lunches. Freeze excess if the batch is especially large.
